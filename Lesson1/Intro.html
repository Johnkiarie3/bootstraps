FEMINISM IN KENYA

What is feminism?

1.) Feminism has also been defined as an ideology that seeks equality of both men and women. It deconstructs patriarchy and promotes gender equality. It also advocates the transformation of all social relations of power that oppress, exploit or marginalize any set of people‐ women and men, on the basis of their gender, age, sexual orientation, ability, race, religion, nationality, location, ability, class, caste or ethnicity.
2.)Feminism appeared, for the first time, as early as the 19th Century, the century of large emancipatory, social movements, as an international social movement with an autonomous identity with theoretical and organizational structure, central to other important social movements, including various socialism and anarchism.

Feminism is a diverse, competing and often opposing collection of social theories, political movements and moral philosophies largely motivated by or concerning the experience of women, especially in terms of their social, political and economic inequalities. One institutionally predominant type of feminism focuses on limiting or eliminating gender inequality to promote women’s rights, interests and issues in society.
